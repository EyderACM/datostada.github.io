About Datostada

It is a free international meeting for society, academia, government and industry, about the role of data in several high-impact topics, and how people can take advantage from it, in order to prosecute and disrupt the scientific and technological potential in the southeast of the country and the neighboring regions.

Parque Científico Tecnológico de Yucatán, Mérida, YUC

Yucatán is on the verge of becoming, as well as a center of cultural and natural tourism, a place of scientific formation with technology at the forefront. The Scientific Technology Park of Yucatan is a conceived center for the promotion of science and the development of environmental technologies and alternative energies. It currently houses renowned academic institutions including the National Autonomous University of Mexico (UNAM), the Center for Research in Mathematics (CIMAT) and the Center for Research and Advanced Studies of the National Polytechnic Institute (CINVESTAV), as well as high level laboratories and businesses related to tech development.

Benjamín Sánchez-Lengeling

Meet our guest international speaker!

About Him

Benjamín earned his PhD at Harvard University under the supervision of Professor Alán Aspuru-Guzik, and currently he works at Google Brain as a Research Scientist. His research centers around using machine learning techniques to build data-driven models for prediction of molecular properties and generation of new molecules and materials via generative models. Applications include solar cells, solubility, drug-design, and smelly molecules.

For him, education has been truly a transformative experience. He dedicates part of his time to two projects of which he is a contributor and also founding member: Clubes de Ciencia México and RIIAA Conference, a summer school and ML conference in Mexico.
